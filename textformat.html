Para One

The sun rises every morning, spreading its golden rays across the sky. People often start their day by admiring the beautiful sunrise, which symbolizes hope and positivity . Farmers depend on the sunlight for their crops , and scientists study its energy to understand the mysteries of the universe. Without the sun, life on Earth would not be possible.

Para Four

The Himalayas are the highest mountain range in the world, stretching across India, Nepal, Bhutan, and Tibet. They are home to many sacred temples and picturesque valleys. Climbers from all over the world dream of conquering Mount Everest, the highest peak on Earth. The Himalayas also play a vital role in regulating the climate and providing fresh water sources.

Para Five

Water is one of the most important substances on Earth. Its chemical formula is H2O, which means it contains two hydrogen atoms and one oxygen atom. Another simple compound is carbon dioxide, represented as CO2. Plants use CO2 and H 2O during photosynthesis to make glucose (C6H12 O6 ) and oxygen (O2 ).

Para Six

One of the most common formulas in mathematics is the area of a rectangle:
Area = length × width.
For example, if the length = 6 cm and the width = 4 cm, the area = 6 × 4 = 24 cm2. Similarly, the formula for the perimeter of a rectangle is: Perimeter = 2 × (length + width).
