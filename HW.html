Sanjay Gandhi National Park (SGNP) is home to diverse wildlife, including various mammals such as leopards, spotted deer, sambar, monkeys, and porcupines. The park also supports a rich avian population with over 274 bird species, including kingfishers, parakeets, and sunbirds. Additionally, SGNP is home to many reptiles and amphibians, as well as a wide variety of butterflies and insects. These caves served as a prominent Buddhist monastic center for over a thousand years, dating from the 1st century BCE to the 10th century CE. They were used by Buddhist monks for study, meditation, and worship. The complex comprises 109 caves carved out of a massive basalt outcrop, showcasing impressive rock-cut architecture. This includes Chaityas (prayer halls with stupas), Viharas (monastic residences), and a sophisticated water management system. The caves are adorned with numerous sculptures, relief carvings, and remnants of paintings depicting Buddhist themes, including large statues of Buddha and Bodhisattvas like Avalokitesvara. Location and Accessibility: Situated within the lush Sanjay Gandhi National Park, the caves offer a serene environment and are accessible via road, railway (Borivali station is nearest), and even cycling within the park.
